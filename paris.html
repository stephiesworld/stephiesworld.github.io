Paris

a personal guide

Selected places

Fitness

Burning Bar

42 Avenue Raymond Poincaré · 16th arr.

Hot pilates, afrohouse music, mood lighting, French-speaking instructors. My favorite workout class in the entire world. I don’t say that lightly.

Museums

Musée Rodin

77 Rue de Varenne · 7th arr.

One of the most peaceful museums in Paris, with a garden that does most of the heavy lifting. Plan to stay longer than you think.

Fondation Louis Vuitton

8 Avenue du Mahatma Gandhi · Bois de Boulogne

My favorite art museum I’ve ever visited. They only show one exhibit at a time, and each level traces the arc of the artist’s life — the inspirations, the catalysts, the turning points. You leave understanding not just what someone made, but why.

Restaurants

Au P’tit Curieux

27 Rue de Sambre-et-Meuse · 10th arr.

My best friend Mackenzie and I have had multiple four-hour dinners here — sometimes twice in the same trip. The owner is funny and charming. One of those places you come back to every visit without even deciding to.

Cojean

28 Rue du Bac · 7th arr.

Healthy bowls clearly marked with protein content. If I lived in Paris full time, this would be my daily fast casual. Multiple locations across the city.

Parks

Jardin du Luxembourg

6th arr.

The most beautiful park in the world, and I will stand by that. Tennis courts on site, which is just unfair to every other park in existence.

Shopping

Citypharma

26 Rue du Four · 6th arr.

The French pharmacy experience is non-negotiable. Go in without a list, leave with fifteen things you didn’t know you needed. I’ve spent more money here than I’ll admit.

From Future

Le Marais · multiple locations

My favorite sweater shop in the world. Delightful, colorful cashmere in the most charming little store. You will leave with something.

Explore

Rue du Bac

7th arr.

Walk the whole thing. One of the most charming streets in Paris — great shops, a good energy, and my first Paris address was number 52.

Saint-Germain-des-Prés

6th arr.

My favorite arrondissement. The area around the main square has everything — little restaurants, great shops, the right kind of crowd. Just walk around.

The Seine at Sunset

Les Berges de la Seine

You have to go for a run here during sunset. One of the best running routes in the world — and yes, I am completely biased about this city.

A few universal rules: every boulangerie is the right boulangerie.
Every coffee is fine. Every meal will probably be good.
Every bookstore is an adventure — being beautiful is mandatory in Paris.
Paris has very low floors and very high ceilings.
